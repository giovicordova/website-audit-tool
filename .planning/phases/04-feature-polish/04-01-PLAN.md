**In SKILL.md, add to Phase A (after the curl robots.txt step, item 1):**

Add an instruction block titled "AI Crawler Policy Analysis" that tells Claude to:

1. Parse the robots.txt content fetched in step 1 for AI bot rules
2. Check against this canonical bot list (embed the full table from 04-RESEARCH.md with all 14 bots, their provider, type training/retrieval, and purpose)
3. For each bot, classify as:
   - **Blocked**: Has its own `User-agent` section with `Disallow: /`, OR falls under `User-agent: *` with `Disallow: /` and has NO specific override
   - **Allowed**: Has its own `User-agent` section without `Disallow: /`, OR has an explicit `Allow` directive, OR no blocking rule applies
   - **Unaddressed**: Not mentioned in robots.txt at all (no specific rule, no wildcard coverage)
4. IMPORTANT: Specific `User-agent` rules override wildcard (`*`) rules. A bot with its own section is governed by that section only.
5. Also check for any `User-agent` entries containing "AI", "bot", "crawler", "spider" that are NOT in the canonical list -- report these as "Other AI bots detected"
6. Grade using this rubric:
   - A: Training bots blocked, retrieval bots allowed, no major bots unaddressed
   - B: Most bots addressed, 1-2 unaddressed
   - C: Some bots addressed but significant gaps or inconsistencies
   - D: Only 1-2 bots addressed, most unaddressed
   - F: No AI bot rules in robots.txt at all
7. This is informational with a letter grade -- NOT part of the weighted overall score
